०२ — The Loom

Fifty years at the loom,
one thread at a time.

VASTRA began in 1974 as a single handloom in a courtyard in Patan. Today we work with weaving families across Palpa, Terhathum and the Kathmandu valley — the historic homes of dhaka cloth — and herders in Mustang whose chyangra goats give the world's finest pashmina.

Nothing here is printed. The geometry you see in every topi and cholo is counted into the cloth, pick by pick, by hands that learned the pattern from their mothers. A single daura can carry forty thousand intersections of thread.

०३ — The Craft

From ridge-line to hem

क

Shear & Spin

High-pasture wool and chyangra down, combed by hand each spring above 4,000 metres, spun on drop spindles on the walk back down.

ख

Root & Dye

Madder red, walnut black, true indigo. Our dye house uses river water, mountain lichen and zero synthetic fixative.

ग

Count & Weave

Dhaka geometry is never drawn — it is counted from memory. Two centimetres of pattern an hour. We refuse to hurry it.
